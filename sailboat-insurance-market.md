<p><strong>The Sailboat Insurance Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Actual Cash Value</li><li>Agreed Amount Value</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Sailboat insurance typically falls into two main market types: Actual Cash Value and Agreed Amount Value. Actual Cash Value policies typically pay out based on the current market value of the sailboat at the time of the loss. Agreed Amount Value policies, on the other hand, involve the policyholder and insurer agreeing on a set value for the sailboat upfront, which will be paid out in the event of a covered loss. Both market types offer different levels of coverage and premiums based on the agreed terms.</p></p>
