<p><p>Aircraft thermal management systems can be categorized into two main types: Air Cycle and Vapor Cycle systems. Air Cycle systems utilize compressed air to cool and regulate the temperature inside the aircraft cabin, while Vapor Cycle systems use a refrigerant to achieve the same purpose. Both systems play a crucial role in maintaining a comfortable and safe environment for passengers and crew members during flight. The Air Cycle system is typically more energy-efficient, while the Vapor Cycle system offers better cooling capabilities.</p></p>
