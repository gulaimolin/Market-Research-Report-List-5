<p><p>Fixed optical mounts are essential components used in optical experiments and setups for securely holding optical elements such as mirrors, lenses, and filters. There are two main types of fixed optical mounts: cage mountable and post mountable. Cage mountable mounts are designed to be easily integrated into standard optical cage systems, providing flexibility and modularity in optical setups. Post mountable mounts, on the other hand, are designed to be directly mounted onto optical tables or breadboards, providing stability and precision for optical components. Both types serve the purpose of securely holding optical elements in place for accurate and reliable performance in optical experiments.</p></p>
