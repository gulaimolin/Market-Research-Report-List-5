<p><p>Voltage variable attenuators are used in electronic circuits to control the level of signal attenuation based on the applied voltage. There are two main types of voltage variable attenuators: diode-based attenuators and MMIC-based attenuators. Diode-based attenuators use diodes to vary the attenuation level, while MMIC-based attenuators utilize Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuit technology for precise and efficient control. Both types offer different advantages and are used in various applications depending on the specific requirements of the circuit.</p></p>
