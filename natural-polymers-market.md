<p><p>Natural polymers can be classified into various types based on their sources and properties. Cellulose ethers are derived from cellulose, providing stability and viscosity in various industries. Exudate and vegetable gums are obtained from plant sources, offering thickening and gelling properties. Starch and fermentation products are derived from plant sources as well, serving as biodegradable and renewable alternatives. Other natural polymers include proteins, alginates, chitosan, and pectin, each with unique characteristics and applications in various industries.</p></p>
