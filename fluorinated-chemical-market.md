<p><p>Fluorinated chemicals can be classified into two main types - inorganic and organic. Inorganic fluorinated chemicals are derived from minerals and include compounds like hydrofluoric acid and sodium fluoride. These chemicals are commonly used in industries such as glass manufacturing and metal processing. Organic fluorinated chemicals, on the other hand, are carbon-based compounds that contain fluorine atoms. They are widely used in products such as pharmaceuticals, pesticides, and refrigerants due to their unique properties like chemical stability and water repellency.</p></p>
