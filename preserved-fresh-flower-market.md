<p><p>Preserved fresh flowers are typically processed using various drying methods such as air drying, freeze drying, silica gel drying, and others. Air drying involves hanging flowers upside down in a dry and warm location until they dry out. Freeze drying involves freezing the flowers and then removing the moisture through sublimation. Silica gel drying requires placing the flowers in silica gel crystals to absorb the moisture. Other methods may include glycerin preservation or pressing flowers. Each method offers unique benefits and results in preserved flowers that retain their natural beauty.</p></p>
