<p><p>Industrial digital cameras are classified into two main types based on the image sensor technology used - CMOS and CCD. CMOS cameras are cost-effective, consume less power, and offer faster frame rates, making them suitable for high-speed applications. On the other hand, CCD cameras provide higher image quality, better sensitivity, and lower noise levels, making them ideal for applications that require superior image performance. Both types of cameras have their own strengths and weaknesses, catering to different industrial needs and preferences.</p></p>
