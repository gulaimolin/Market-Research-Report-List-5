<p><p>Linear accelerators are medical devices used for radiation therapy in cancer treatment. They are classified based on the energy levels they produce. Low-energy linear accelerators are typically used for superficial tumors and have energy levels ranging from 4 to 10 MeV. On the other hand, high-energy linear accelerators are used for deeper-seated tumors and have energy levels above 10 MeV. Both types of linear accelerators are crucial in providing targeted radiation therapy to cancer patients, offering precise and effective treatment options.</p></p>
