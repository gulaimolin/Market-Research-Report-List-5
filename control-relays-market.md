<p><p>Control relays are devices used in electrical circuits to control the operation of other devices. Impulse relays are used to control devices that require a momentary signal to function. Monitoring relays are designed to continuously monitor specific parameters, such as current or voltage, and activate or deactivate devices based on predefined conditions. Signal conditioning relays are used to modify signals to make them compatible with other devices in a circuit. Each type of relay serves a specific purpose in controlling and monitoring electrical systems.</p></p>
